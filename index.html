Artificial Intelligence and Blockchain Lab

We build and study trustworthy AI systems and decentralized infrastructure. Our work spans NLP and large language models, blockchain protocols, and privacy-preserving computation — with a practical focus on real systems that handle sensitive data.

What we care about

AI is moving from “tools” to “agents”. Blockchain is moving from “coins” to “infrastructure”. We study what happens when these meet.

  • Trust: who must be honest, and what can be verified?
  • Privacy: can we learn from data we are not allowed to see?
  • Reliability: can systems stay live under real participation constraints?
  • Deployment: can research survive contact with production?

Research areas

We work at the intersection of language, security, and decentralized systems.

Open to academic and industry collaborations.

NLP & Large Language Models

LLM-based systems for understanding, summarization, classification, and decision support — including evaluation under real constraints.

Trustworthy AI

Safety, robustness, and accountability of AI systems — especially when they act across organizations and sensitive workflows.

Blockchain protocols & architectures

Design and analysis of decentralized protocols: trust models, liveness assumptions, threat modeling, and protocol engineering.

Privacy-preserving computation

Secure computation techniques enabling collaboration without data exposure: MPC-style thinking, verifiable processing, and secure system design.

AI + Blockchain integration

Architectures where decentralized infrastructure supports transparent AI pipelines: provenance, auditability, and incentive-aware coordination.

Digital public infrastructure

Applied cryptographic systems for high-stakes domains — governance, voting, compliance — with measurable trust assumptions.

How we work

Engineering-driven: formalize assumptions, quantify trade-offs, validate with real deployments.

Systems that are both useful and defensible.

Assumption-first design

We make hidden assumptions explicit: threat models, trust boundaries, and liveness conditions — then design around them.

Measure what matters

Instead of “secure-ish”, we strive for measurable security and trust properties with clear failure modes.

Real-world constraints

We care about what survives production: UX constraints, incentive pressure, ops reality, and adversarial behavior.
